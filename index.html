Why is your DNA shaped like a folded ribbon?

In cellular biology, DNA isn't a long, straight ladder floating freely in space. Instead, over two metres of genetic code is meticulously folded inside the microscopic nucleus of every cell in a complex 3D looped system known as chromatin architecture (a folded nucleosome fiber).

These 3D loops bring distant genes into physical contact with one another so your cells can efficiently read instructions.
