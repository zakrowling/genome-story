Why is your DNA shaped like a folded ribbon?

In cellular biology, DNA isn't a long, straight ladder floating freely in space. Instead, over two metres of genetic code is meticulously folded inside the microscopic nucleus of every cell in a complex 3D looped system known as chromatin architecture.

These 3D loops bring distant genes into physical contact with one another so your cells can efficiently read instructions.

How to Explore & Navigate

Rotate & Tilt 🖱️ Click and drag anywhere on the 3D canvas to spin the genome.
Zoom In / Out 🔍 Use your mouse scroll wheel or pinch on a trackpad.
Fly-To Gene Locus 📍 Click any "Explore" button on the left panel to zoom directly to that gene locus.
Reset View 🔄 Click "Reset View" at the bottom of the panel to return to full overview mode.
